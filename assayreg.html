What’s the problem?

Research groups often do not have a consistent way of recording data for a given project or assay, with even small changes in data field names leading to confusion and delays in locating assay data. This can lead to duplication of effort. Where data standards are not applied consistently, this means that it can be difficult to pull together data for reports or publications, and the effort to bring new lab members up to date with the history of the group’s research is greatly increased.

Many research groups still have a paper lab notebook as their only way of recording assay data results and method development, rather than a simple, secure system demanded by most funding bodies to comply with data management mandates. Non-digital methods of storing assay data also mean it can be daunting to submit protocols to public databases such as PubChem. ChemBio Hub aims to make this process more straightforward.

What is ChemBio Hub AssayReg?

AssayReg allows groups to manage project assay data in a sensible structured way, with multiple projects, sub projects and assay types, ordered in a hierarchical structure. You can upload files containing assay or activity data, preview your data before saving, then immediately view the data in context of other uploaded data. You can also add data one item at a time through the built-in forms. Project data is stored securely and your administrators can define who has access to your project to create, view and update the assay data, as well as what information is needed as a minimum for each data level.

The ChemBio Hub team can provide curation support to ensure data is recorded consistently and in line with the standard repositories. This greatly simplifies the process of depositing data into ChEMBL and PubChem and ensures that preparing data for publication is also much easier.

You will be able to demonstrate to funding bodies that your data management policy is robust and fully compliant with their requirements, so reducing the risk of having a grant application fail on this vital requirement.
